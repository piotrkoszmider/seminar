This series of talks will be devoted to the Banach algebra M(T) of measures on the circle with the convolution of measures. This was the topic of Przemys³aw Ohrysko's M.A. project conducted at Warsaw University under the guidance of Micha³ Wojciechowski and concluded this year. Przemysław focused on the Wienner-Pitt phenomenon.


Our purpose would be to try to represent the multiplicative functionals on this Banach algebra in a combinatorial way. Like representing multiplicative funtionals on the Banach algebra ell-infty naturally leads to the points of the Cech-Stone compactification of the integers, i.e., ultraflters of subsets of the inegers, we expect here some related set-theoretic constructions.
The talks are designed to nonspecialists who want to learn about the beautiful subject and perhaps try to think about a better representation of the functionals mentioned above. To get acquainted with the subject you may want to have a look at the M.A. thesis of Przemysław Ohrysko.

Talks in the first semester of 2011-12.

Time and place: Thursdays 11.15- 13.30, room 105, Sniadeckich 8

The scope of the seminar: Set-theoretic methods in diverse fields of mathematics, in particular in Banach spaces, C*-algebras, topology, the structure of the real line, measure theory, Boolean algebras, topological groups. Here we include both the developing of such methods as forcing, descriptive set theory, Ramsey theory as well as their concrete applications in the fields mentioned above.

Working group style: We will make efforts so that this seminar has more a working character rather than the presentation style. This means that we encourage long digressions, discussions, background preparations and participation of everyone. To achive this, the duration of the seminar will not be rigidly fixed and may reach even 3 hours with the breaks. We would like to immerse ourselves into the details of the mathematical arguments studied. We hope that such a seminar may complement presentations at the Wednesday set theory seminar at the University of Warsaw.
